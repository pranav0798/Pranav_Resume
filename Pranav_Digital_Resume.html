The Beginning

The Story So Far

Five years ago, I started as a Data Operations Associate. Today, I lead enterprise investigations and build tools that save teams hundreds of hours. Here is how that happened.

I work at the intersection of digital forensics and data analytics — collecting, processing, and querying data at a scale where a single oversight can become a very expensive mistake. Over 5+ years at Consilio LLC, including a client secondment supporting Barclays' enterprise data collection function, I have built a reputation for spotting process inefficiencies, data-quality issues, and automation opportunities before they spiral. I caught a duplicate-ingestion issue that had inflated a dataset to 4TB — reducing it to 500GB and cutting downstream processing time and review cost before it reached the review stage. I eliminated a manual renaming process that was quietly costing the team hours every week. When our commercial file-transfer solution could not meet operational requirements, I built an internal Robocopy wrapper and later transformed it into a production-ready C# WinForms application with multithreading, pause/resume, ETA estimation, and automated reporting.

I am comfortable owning ambiguous data problems end-to-end — SQL and Excel for the mechanics, clear documentation for the audit trail, and enough cross-functional coordination to get global stakeholders aligned on the fix.
